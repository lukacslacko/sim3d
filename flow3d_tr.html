flow3d_tr (GPU) — V = ρ·T (unit time-direction T, density ρ)

Same energy as flow3d.html, with the mass-current decomposed V = ρ·T, |T| = 1. Then |∇V|² = |∇ρ|² + ρ²|∇T|² (cross-term vanishes because T·∂T = ½∂(T·T) = 0), so E = ∫ [½|∇ρ|² + ½ρ²|∇T|² − Φρ + (1/8πG)|∇Φ|²] with ∇²Φ = −κρ. Gradient flow:
  dρ/dt = ν·∇²ρ − ρ·|∇T|² + g·Φ (diffusion, defect-evacuates-mass, gravity)
  dT/dt = ρ²·∇²T + 2ρ·(∇ρ·∇T), then re-normalise T ← T/|T|. The −ρ|∇T|² term should evacuate mass from T-defect regions and pile it up in the smooth-T regions in between — i.e. potential channels. The unit-length constraint on T gives the field a topological character (T : T³ → S²) that V ∈ ℝ³ didn't have, so smoothing is bounded below by the topology of the initial map.

       
No ∇·V = 0 projection in this variant — mass is conserved if "conserve mass" is on (rescales ρ to its Randomize-time total each step). In the cube boundary mode, ρ and Φ are zero outside but T on the ghost cells is the unit radial vector from the cube centre, so stencils at the wall pull T toward radial-outward over time (the cube becomes a "star" with outward time-direction at infinity).
