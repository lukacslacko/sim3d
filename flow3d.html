flow3d (GPU) — sourceless mass-flow with self-gravity

Same physics as the CPU version: gradient descent on E = ∫ [½|∇V|² − Φ|V| + (1/8πG)|∇Φ|²] subject to ∇·V = 0, with ∇²Φ = −κ|V| on the periodic N³ torus. All per-cell stencils — the Φ Jacobi sweeps, the force evaluation F = ν∇²V + g·Φ·V̂, the addition V ← V + dt·F, the divergence, the χ Jacobi sweeps for the Helmholtz projection, the gradient subtract V ← V − ∇χ, and the |V| reductions for mean-of-|V| and total mass — run as WebGL2 fragment shaders on a 2D atlas (an Nt×Nt grid of N×N slices, Nt = ⌈√N⌉) of RGBA32F textures. Only V and Φ are read back to CPU per step, and only for the slice / streamline visualisations.

       
Higher N stays smooth on the GPU; the only round-trip is a 1×1 readback per reduction (mean of |V|, total mass) plus per-frame V,Φ readback for visualisation. Shift is render-only (PBC wraps); use the shift x/y/z sliders or press x/y/z (Shift+key reverses) to translate the cube origin by one cell along that axis. Refine ×2 doubles N and continues the simulation from the upscaled state.
