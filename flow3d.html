flow3d — sourceless mass-flow with self-gravity

V is a divergence-free 3D mass-current field on a periodic N×N×N torus (direction = local time-arrow, length = mass density). Φ is a scalar gravitational potential satisfying ∇²Φ = −κ|V| on the torus. We do gradient descent on E[V,Φ] = ∫ [½|∇V|² − Φ|V| + (1/8πG)|∇Φ|²] d³x subject to ∇·V = 0. Each step: (1) solve ∇²Φ = −κ·(|V|−⟨|V|⟩) by Jacobi; (2) V ← V + dt·(ν·∇²V + g·Φ·V̂) with regularised V̂ = V/√(|V|²+ε²); (3) Helmholtz-project V to be div-free (∇²χ = ∇·V, V ← V − ∇χ); (4) optionally rescale Σ|V| to its Randomize value. The Dirichlet term ½|∇V|² resists infinite concentration; the −Φ|V| term is the gravitational instability that condenses streamlines into channels; ∇·V = 0 plus torus topology keeps streamlines closed.

     
Try increasing g (or making it negative) and watch streamlines bunch up. Higher κ sharpens Φ for a given mass distribution. Step size dt should scale roughly as 1/N² because Φ scales like |V|·N²; if the energy / Σ|V| grows or oscillates, lower dt.
