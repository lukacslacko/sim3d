2D gravity — sourceless mass-flow on a torus

M is a sourceless vector field whose direction is the local arrow-of-time and whose length is mass. Φ is a scalar potential satisfying ∇²Φ = |M| − ⟨|M|⟩; the potential vector field is P = −∇Φ, so P points toward concentrations of |M| with magnitude growing roughly as distance × mass. Each step bends M perpendicular to itself toward P (preserving |M|), then projects M back onto the divergence-free subspace via a Helmholtz / pressure projection. Edges wrap.

 
Bend rotates M in the plane toward P (perp component) so |M| is conserved. Parallel pull additionally projects P onto M (≠ 0 lets matter accumulate or burn off). Damping shrinks |M| each step. div-free / potential iters control Jacobi-relaxation depth.
