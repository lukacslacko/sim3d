3D gravity — sourceless mass-flow with prescribed curl

M is a 3D mass-flow field on a periodic N×N×N torus, kept sourceless (∇·M = 0); ω is now a vector curl field and we want curl M = ω. Each step: (1) ωtarget(q) = κ · Σp∈disk(q,R) [m(p) × (q−p)] · w(|q−p|/R) / |q−p|² where w is a cubic-smoothstep window with cutoff R = cutoff·N; (2) ω relaxes toward ωtarget; (3) curl(M) is pushed toward ω componentwise via vector potential — solve −∇²A = ω − curl(M) by Jacobi, then M ← M + rate · ∇×A; (4) Helmholtz-project M to be div-free; (5) rescale M so Σ|M| matches its Randomize value. The streamline view rotates with yaw/pitch sliders. Slice panels show one orthogonal slice (axis & index selectable) of various derived fields.

     
Kernel cost is O(N³·R³). At N=20, cutoff 0.25, that's ≈4 Mops/step (smooth); at N=32 it's ~70 Mops (≈3 fps); N up to 48 supported but slow per step. Direction colour map for slices: each component of M maps to bias from gray — red ↔ x, green ↔ y, blue ↔ z (positive lightens toward axis colour, negative toward complement).
